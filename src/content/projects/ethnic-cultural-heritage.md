## Lithuanian Ethnic Cultural Heritage in the Photoarchives

An eight-volume publication of albums and CD-ROMs / internet albums (English, Lithuanian, French), 1999–2005. Editor-in-Chief: Prof. Antanas Buračas, with consulting and co-editing by Birutė Kulnytė (Director of the Lithuanian National Museum), Prof. Vacys Milius and Prof. Angelė Vyšniauskaitė.

The series evaluates the national cultural heritage — archaic traditions, latent symbols of ethnic experience, and sacred relics of a pre-Christian understanding of Providence surviving across the ages — from the viewpoint and criteria of the information revolution of the 21st century. The expected eight volumes draw on about **10,000 photographs** from the unique archive of **Balys Buračas** (first half of the 20th century).

### Volumes
- **Vol. I.** *The Sacred Arts in Lithuania* (Lithuanian & English), Vilnius, 640 p., 1999 — also on CD and online.
- **Vol. II.** *Lithuanian Festivities & Customs*, ed. Prof. Angelė Vyšniauskaitė et al.
- **Vol. III.** *Semantics of Textile Weaves*, ed. Dr. Libertas Klimka et al., ~400 p.
- **Vol. IV.** *Mystics of National Mounds*, ed. Dr. Nijolė Tumėnienė & Dr. Zabiela.
- **Vol. V.** *The Ethnic Architecture of Small Towns and Villages.*
- **Vol. VI.** *Folk Works and Their Traditions.*
- **Vol. VII.** *Cultural and Religious Traditions and Heritage of Ethnic Minorities in Lithuania.*

### Judaica · Litvak sacred culture
The heritage also documents Litvak sacred culture, including the synagogue photography of Balys Buračas held in international Jewish-heritage archives (JewishGen, ShtetLinks and others).

All volumes are intended to be available as publications, internet albums and CDs. Proposals for support or joint editing and distribution are welcome.

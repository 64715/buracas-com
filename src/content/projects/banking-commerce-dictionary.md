## Reference Dictionary of Banking and Commerce

**English–Lithuanian–English**, edited (in 6 volumes) by Prof. Antanas Buračas. Some **57,000** financial, banking, commerce, management and real-estate terms and definitions, also codes of currencies and measures, and business abbreviations.

The Reference Dictionary covers finance, micro- and macroeconomics, economic statistics, civil law (financial, contractual, intellectual and other property), state and constitutional law, political economy, and informatics — including terms from the main economic classificators of the UN, World Bank, IMF, ILO, the European Union and other international organizations.

### Volumes
- **Vol. I.** *The Encyclopedic Dictionary of Banking and Commerce*, Lithuanian–English, Vilnius University, 464 p., 1997.
- **Vol. II.** *The Reference Dictionary of World Currencies and Coins*, Vilnius & Vytautas Magnus Universities, 368 p., 1999.
- **Vol. III.** *The International Finance and Commercial Abbreviations*, Mykolas Romeris University, Vilnius, 2008.
- **Vol. IV.** *The European Union Abbreviations in Finance and Economics*, Vilnius, 2008 (in editing).
- **Vol. V.** *The Finance and Investment Information on the Web*, 2006, 530 p.
- **Vol. VI.** *The International Business Tickers and Financial Investment Codes*, Vilnius, ~1,700 p., 2009 (in final review).

Volumes I–III are also available on CD-ROM, named **FINLED** (software by V. Statulevičius & D. Kudžma), convenient for use directly within the environment of working documents.

### Supplements
The Dictionary includes notes and coins of the world's states with their SWIFT codes; tables for calculating simple, compound and discount interest rates; business, banking, exchange and monetary-policy abbreviations and the main international classificators (SNA, EUROVOC, ISIC, CPC, CPA, COICOP, COFOG, NACE, PRODCOM, SITC, MGFS, ISCO and others); official names of central banks and commodity and stock exchanges; metric-to-imperial conversions; a classification of countries by investment-risk rating; and widely used colloquial monetary and banking terms.

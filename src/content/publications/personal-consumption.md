## Abstract

Macroeconomic sustainable equilibrium under dynamic optimised development is analysed theoretically, first of all with account of long-run statistical trends in personal consumption expenditures in highly developed industrial countries. The **asymmetrical effect** between growing and diminishing rates of the satisfaction of personal needs was discovered, for the first time, on the basis of this empirical statistical material.

The factors of the continuing adaptation of consumer behaviour to resource and technological restrictions when satisfying new, prospective needs are analysed, with new conclusions on the specific influence of the intellectualisation of information society at the end of the 20th and beginning of the 21st centuries. The asymmetrical effect may be statistically revealed in the interconnected dynamics of personal incomes and consumer expenses, and in the transition from lowest to highest income groups and vice versa.

## Abstract

The commensurability of developing strategies of socio-economic preferences is considered within the regional integrated imitation of ecological reproduction. The balanced regional multisectoral forecasting practice and the international programme under the author's management are generalised, with account of tasks for the preservation of natural resources and the neutralisation of negative anthropogenic influence.

Regional management strategies developed on the basis of numerical alternative modelling take into account the effects of the utilisation and regeneration of renewable natural resources, as well as the dynamic optimisation of anthropogenic activity under conflicting, interdependent social objectives. Special attention is given to the influence of modified institutional and organisational infrastructures and of a longer time horizon of integrated ecological and socio-economic forecasting.
